A quiet interface, loud on craftsmanship.

Warm, trustworthy, typographically precise. Photography carries the colour; the interface itself stays in bone and ink. Monospace for signposting, a serif for feeling, a clean sans for work.

§ 01 — Colour

Bone, ink, and one warm ember.

The palette is almost monochrome. A warm off-white replaces cold white so photography sits on it like a print on a mat; a deep warm charcoal replaces pure black so text reads softer at long distances. A single terracotta accent is used sparingly — underlines, notification dots, one italic flourish in the hero.

§ 02 — Typography

A serif for feeling, a sans for work, a mono for signposting.

Newsreader (a warm, slightly literary serif with an optical-size axis) handles displays. Geist — clean, modern, humane — handles interface and body. Geist Mono handles eyebrow labels, metadata, captions. Negative tracking on everything display-size or body; positive on mono.

§ 03 — Space & shape

An 8-point rhythm. Pill geometry for action.

Every measure snaps to the eight. Radii stay soft — 4, 8, 14, 24, full pill. Sharp corners are reserved for photography masks and hairlines. Section padding scales with the viewport so the gallery breathes.

§ 04 — Buttons

Pill-shaped, always. Four states, one voice.

Every interactive element is a pill or a circle. Focus rings are dashed, 1.5px, 3px offset — a quiet reference to a builder’s chalk-line. Icons sit to the right of the label with a ten-pixel gap.
